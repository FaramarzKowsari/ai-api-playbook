Engineering Modern Intelligence Through APIs

A Visual Engineering Guide to Multimodal AI, Agents, RAG, Evaluation, Governance, and Real-World Products

This visual technical book explains how modern AI capabilities become reliable systems. It connects API architecture, structured outputs, tool use, agents, retrieval-augmented generation, multimodal computing, evaluation, governance, security, observability, cost control, and commercially useful products.

The publication moves beyond isolated prompts and demonstrations. It provides a production-oriented map for designing, evaluating, securing, governing, and commercializing AI systems across text, documents, images, audio, speech, realtime interaction, and video.

About Faramarz Kowsari

Faramarz Kowsari is an author, Software Engineer and AI researcher based in Istanbul. His work connects Artificial Intelligence, software architecture, data science, technical education, visual knowledge design, and open-source development.

He is the author of AI APIs in Practice, a visual engineering guide to multimodal AI, intelligent agents, retrieval-augmented generation, evaluation, governance, security, observability, and production-ready AI products. The book is independently archived and citable through DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21431944 and is also available through Google Books.

He also designed and developed the companion AI API Playbook, an open-source research-software project containing executable examples, provider-neutral abstractions, tests, notebooks, safety utilities, evaluation workflows, and commercial product blueprints. The software has its own permanent record: DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21419466.

Across more than 80 digital titles published on international platforms, his broader areas of work include prompt engineering, algorithmic trading, educational technology, classical literature, mindfulness, web-based learning tools, and specialized instructional media.
